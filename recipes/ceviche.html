Ceviche

Description

Ceviche is a South American seafood dish from the modern day country of Peru, typically made from fresh raw fish cured in fresh citrus juices, most commonly lemon or lime, but historically made with the juice of bitter orange. It is also spiced with ají, chili peppers or other seasonings and chopped onions, salt, and coriander are also added. The name originates from the Quechuan word siwichi, which means fresh or tender fish.

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ pounds very fresh and high quality fish filets corvina, halibut, escolar, hamachi, mahi-mahi
  • 1 red onion thinly sliced
  • 1 cup freshly squeezed lime juice from about 35- 40 key limes, or 15-20 Peruvian limes
  • 1-2 habanero peppers cut in half, without seeds and deveined
  • 2-3 sprigs of fresh cilantro
  • Salt to taste
  • Finely chopped cilantro to taste

Steps

  1. Step 1

    2. Cut the fish into small cubes, place in a glass bowl and cover with cold water and 1 tablespoon of salt, cover and refrigerate while you prepare the onions and juice the limes.

  2. Step 2

    3. Rub the thin onion slices with 1/2 tablespoon of salt and rinse in cold water.

  3. Step 3

    4. Rinse the fish to remove the salt

  4. Step 4

    5. Place the cubes of fish, half of the sliced onions, and hot peppers in a glass bowl and pour the lime juice over the ingredients. Sprinkle with a little bit of salt. To minimize the acidity of the limes you can put a few ice cubes in the mix.

  5. Step 5

    6. Cover and refrigerate for about 5-15 minutes.

  6. Step 6

    7. Remove the cilantro sprigs and the hot peppers from the mix. Taste the fish ceviche and add additional salt if needed.

  7. Step 7

    8. Use a spoon to place the ceviche in each serving bowl, add additional sliced onions to each bowl, sprinkle with finely chopped cilantro, and diced or sliced hot peppers.

  8. Step 8

    9. Serve immediately with your choice of sides and garnishes.
